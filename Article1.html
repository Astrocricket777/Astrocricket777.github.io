R-1 Oceania

The R-1 aircraft, named "Oceania" by it's pilots was a Crealiteenian Aircraft that saw service primarily in the "Kraken" war against the former nation, The Rye Legion. It was designed and manufactured by Henry Kane, this revolutionary craft paved the way for many of Kane's aircraft, eventually forming the Kane Aircraft Association (KAA)

The Oceania was designed and used as an Reconnacance aircraft, and entered service in small incrementations with the Crealiteenian Navy in early 1912. It swiftly became a reputable aircraft by it's pilots, saying the new radial engine, the Hornet 1100, while giving more vibrations, gave a unique profile to it's controllability. It's easy manueverability made it a favirote among many flyers, including Aurthur Zomengo who claimed the world's first air-to-air kill when he shot an enemy pilot with his .22 Swarzchva in the Oceania.

The Oceania served almost exclusivly as a recon aircraft, using it's tandem seating, the navigator would grab a camera of choice and take pictures over enemy territory and would hope to come back in one piece. The Crealiteen Republic sold 110 Modified Oceania's to Archvozsk Empire in 1914; They had their wings modified so that they could be unscrewed and rescrewed into the main fuesuelage for compactness upon a ship.

From the first prototype test-flight in 1908 to the last in 1931, 764 Oceania's were produced in 8 different models.

Devlopment

In 1907, the Chief Commander of the Crealiteenian Air Force (CAF), Fred Artz, published a request for a quick recon aircraft[1], for the CAF had invested into aircraft but were in dire need of such because of the soon-to-come war. He requested a maximum obtainable speed of 125kts (143mph) and a range of 235 miles (147 km). It needed to be able to haul signifigant weight in consideration of a camera.

In September 1907, the CAF signed a contract with Kane[2], who would manufacture and design said aircraft. After the mock-up inspection in November 1907, construction began on a prototype aircraft designated "XV-R1". As the first aircraft Kane designed, he had to take a look at many other aircrafts of the time, he was a physician and taught at a college in 1903[3], which aided in his knowledge of aerodynamics. Kane suggested that a rounder fuesuelage would create less drag due to it's aerodynamic shape [4]. He also emplyed that a Radial Engine would suit the shape. With the help of his brother, they designed the "Hornet 1100" with the 1100 representing the displacement of 11.00L. [5] After a failed first maiden flight due to an tank leak, the XF-R1 took off for the first time on January 12th 1908.

Design

Engine Considerations

The R-1 used the strongest engine of the time, the 350 hp (260 kW) 9-cylinder Wasp 1100 Kane radial. It had a 11.5 ft (3.5 m) wooden propeller which was mounted directly to the engine crank shaft. The propeller was tested numerous times in order to optimize it's extraction of engine power.

Landing Gear And Wings

The landing gear was connected via independent struts that connected to the main fuesuelage. They were built hollow to lose weight in order do give it a suspension feeling. The wheels were .88 ft (.27 m) in diameter and offered good ground controllability on rough surfaces such as the beaches of the Crealiteen Republic.

Camera Mounting

Considering that the R-1 Oceania was a reconnacance aircraft, it was taken with great care choosing the correct camera. If it (the camera) were to heavy, it could dramatically effect the aircraft's performance negativly. It was eventually decided to use the Baylee's Mk.2 Camera which offered very good quality imagery for the time[6]. Early versions of the R-1 did not have a camera mount built into the aircraft itself. Instead, the navigator (the front seater) would bring a camera and take a picture manually by leaning out of the seat. After some fatal incedents with pilots falling out of the aircraft[7], Kane Aeronautics designed the R-1B (Marking the previous as R-1A) which had a built-in camera rail on the belly of the aircraft.
